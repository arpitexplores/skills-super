# Campaign Orchestration

Use this module when the user wants to run a campaign rather than improve one isolated asset. It co-ordinates strategy, creative, channels, tracking, launch readiness, and post-campaign learning.

## When To Use

Use this for requests such as:
- "Plan a product launch campaign."
- "Create a campaign for this offer."
- "Build a campaign calendar."
- "Co-ordinate email, social, ads, and landing page work."
- "Set up campaign tracking and reporting."
- "Create a launch checklist."
- "Review whether this campaign is ready to go live."

If the user asks only for one asset, route directly to the relevant module:
- Landing page or conversion problem: `page-cro.md`
- Copy, ads, CTAs, or brand voice: `copywriting.md`
- Email campaign or nurture flow: `email-sequence.md`
- Social posts or social calendar: `social-content.md`
- Paid media structure or ad optimisation: `paid-ads.md`
- Analytics, UTM governance, or reporting: `analytics-tracking.md`

## Operating Principle

A campaign is a system. Do not produce isolated assets until the offer, audience, success metric, conversion path, and tracking plan are clear enough to avoid wasted execution.

## Minimal Intake

Ask only for missing information. If the user gives partial context, proceed with stated assumptions.

| Area | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Goal | What business outcome should this campaign produce? |
| Audience | Who is the specific buyer, user, or segment? |
| Offer | What are we asking them to do, buy, book, download, or try? |
| Timeline | What is the launch date and campaign duration? |
| Channels | Which channels are in scope: email, social, paid, organic, partners, events, website? |
| Assets | What exists already: landing page, product page, email list, ad account, creative, case studies? |
| Measurement | What counts as success and where will it be measured? |
| Constraints | Any brand, legal, budget, compliance, geography, or approval constraints? |

## Workflow

### 1. Define The Campaign Job

Clarify the campaign type and optimise around the correct job.

| Campaign Type | Primary Job | Typical KPI |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Product launch | Create awareness and initial adoption | Sign-ups, trials, demos, purchases |
| Feature announcement | Drive activation or expansion | Feature usage, upgrades, retained usage |
| Lead generation | Capture qualified demand | Leads, MQLs, cost per lead, lead quality |
| Nurture campaign | Move prospects closer to action | Email engagement, replies, demo requests |
| Re-engagement | Recover dormant users or leads | Reactivation, return visits, conversions |
| Event/webinar | Drive registration and attendance | Registrations, attendance rate, follow-up conversions |
| Promotion | Create time-bound revenue | Purchases, revenue, conversion rate |
| Content campaign | Build authority and future demand | Qualified traffic, subscribers, assisted conversions |

### 2. Build The Campaign Spine

Every campaign needs a spine before channel assets:

1. Audience: who this is for.
2. Problem: what they care about now.
3. Offer: what action they should take.
4. Promise: what outcome they get.
5. Proof: why they should believe it.
6. CTA: the next action.
7. Measurement: how success is judged.

Use this message structure:

```text
For [audience] who struggle with [problem],
this campaign offers [solution/offer],
so they can [specific outcome],
with proof from [evidence],
and the next step is [CTA].
```

### 3. Select Channels By Role

Do not use every channel by default. Assign a job to each channel.

| Channel | Best Role | Common Asset |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Email | Convert known audience and nurture interest | Announcement, sequence, reminder, follow-up |
| Organic social | Build awareness and repeat exposure | Teaser, launch post, proof post, founder note |
| Paid search | Capture active intent | Search ad, comparison landing page |
| Paid social | Create demand and retarget interest | Short-form ad, testimonial ad, offer ad |
| Website/landing page | Explain value and convert | Campaign landing page, pricing CTA, form |
| Blog/content | Educate and support search demand | Pillar article, comparison page, guide |
| Partner/community | Borrow trust and reach | Co-marketing email, community post, webinar |
| Sales/customer success | Convert high-intent or retained accounts | One-pager, talk track, account list |

## Channel Sequencing

### Product Launch Sequence

Use this when the user is launching a product, feature, waitlist, or public release.

| Phase | Timing | Work |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pre-launch | 2-4 weeks before | Audience list, positioning, landing page, tracking, teaser content |
| Warm-up | 1-2 weeks before | Educational content, founder story, proof, waitlist or early access |
| Launch | Launch week | Announcement email, social push, paid/retargeting, partner posts, sales enablement |
| Follow-up | 1-2 weeks after | Objection handling, case study/proof, reminder emails, retargeting |
| Optimisation | During campaign | Improve weak steps, pause poor assets, double down on winners |
| Wrap-up | End of campaign | Performance report, learnings, reusable assets |

### Lead Generation Sequence

| Phase | Work |
| --- | --- |
| Offer design | Define lead magnet, demo reason, consultation angle, or event promise |
| Landing path | Build landing page, form, thank-you page, confirmation email |
| Traffic | Use email, social, paid, partner, or content distribution |
| Nurture | Send welcome/value/proof/CTA sequence |
| Qualification | Score leads by fit, engagement, and conversion behaviour |
| Sales handoff | Provide source, intent, offer, pain point, and recommended follow-up |

### Re-engagement Sequence

| Phase | Work |
| --- | --- |
| Segment | Define dormant users, cold leads, lapsed buyers, or inactive subscribers |
| Diagnose | Identify why they disengaged: timing, value, trust, relevance, friction |
| Reframe | Lead with new value, product improvement, expiry, or helpful content |
| Sequence | Send short sequence with one clear CTA per touch |
| Suppress | Remove non-responders from future pressure campaigns |

Use this table for campaign calendars:

| Date | Channel | Asset | Funnel Stage | Message Pillar | CTA | Owner | Status | Tracking URL |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [date] | [email/social/paid/content] | [asset] | [TOFU/MOFU/BOFU] | [pillar] | [CTA] | [owner] | [draft/review/live] | [URL] |

Calendar rules:
- Put launch-critical assets first.
- Use one primary CTA per asset.
- Reuse core message pillars rather than inventing new angles for every channel.
- Make each channel feel native; do not paste the same copy everywhere.
- Include tracking URLs before approval, not after launch.

## UTM And Tracking Governance

Use consistent UTMs for all campaign traffic that does not already have reliable source attribution.

### Naming Rules

- Use lowercase.
- Use hyphens instead of spaces.
- Keep campaign names stable for the whole campaign.
- Use `utm_content` for creative, placement, or variant.
- Use `utm_term` for paid search keyword or audience where useful.
- Do not mix singular/plural or abbreviations without a documented convention.

### Recommended Fields

| Parameter | Purpose | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `utm_source` | Traffic source or platform | `linkedin`, `newsletter`, `partner-name` |
| `utm_medium` | Channel type | `email`, `paid-social`, `organic-social`, `cpc` |
| `utm_campaign` | Campaign initiative | `q2-product-launch` |
| `utm_content` | Variant or placement | `founder-post`, `hero-cta`, `testimonial-a` |
| `utm_term` | Keyword or audience | `crm-software`, `retargeting-30d` |

### Tracking Checklist

- [ ] Primary conversion event is defined.
- [ ] Thank-you page or success state is tracked.
- [ ] UTMs are applied to email, paid, partner, and social links.
- [ ] Internal links do not use UTMs.
- [ ] Campaign URLs are tested before launch.
- [ ] Reporting view can separate source, medium, campaign, and content.
- [ ] Privacy and consent requirements are respected.

## Brand And Message Quality

Before launch, check:

- The main promise is specific, not vague.
- Claims have proof or are softened.
- The CTA matches the user's stage of awareness.
- The tone matches the channel and audience.
- The campaign uses the same terminology across landing page, email, social, and ads.
- The copy avoids hype, empty superlatives, and unsupported urgency.
- Legal, medical, financial, or regulated claims have appropriate review where relevant.

Use this quick voice check:

| Check | Pass Criteria |
| --- | --- |
| Clarity | A new visitor understands the offer in five seconds. |
| Consistency | The same product, feature, and audience terms are used everywhere. |
| Credibility | Proof supports the biggest claim. |
| Action | The next step is obvious and low-friction. |
| Fit | The tone suits the channel and buyer context. |

## Launch Readiness Checklist

### Strategy

- [ ] Campaign objective is measurable.
- [ ] Audience and segment are clearly defined.
- [ ] Offer, CTA, and conversion path are clear.
- [ ] Timeline, owners, and approvals are assigned.
- [ ] Risks and dependencies are documented.

### Creative

- [ ] Landing page or destination is live/staged.
- [ ] Email copy is drafted and tested.
- [ ] Social posts are prepared for each platform.
- [ ] Paid creative and ad copy match the landing page.
- [ ] Brand voice and terminology are consistent.
- [ ] Images have alt text where applicable.

### Technical

- [ ] Forms, checkout, booking, or signup flow works.
- [ ] Mobile responsiveness is checked.
- [ ] Page speed and Core Web Vitals are acceptable for paid or launch traffic.
- [ ] All links resolve correctly.
- [ ] Tracking pixels/events are firing.
- [ ] UTMs are validated.

### Measurement

- [ ] Primary KPI is visible in the reporting tool.
- [ ] Baseline is recorded.
- [ ] Target is defined.
- [ ] Dashboard/reporting owner is assigned.
- [ ] Reporting cadence is set.

## Campaign Operating Loop

Use this when the user wants an ongoing campaign engine rather than a single launch plan. The same loop can support product launches, content campaigns, partner campaigns, social programmes, newsletters, webinars, and demand generation.

```text
Inputs -> planning -> production -> review -> scheduling -> launch -> monitoring -> learning -> next cycle
```

| Stage | What To Define | Quality Gate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Inputs | Campaign goal, audience, offer, trend signals, customer insights, existing assets | Is there enough context to avoid generic output? |
| Planning | Message pillars, channels, timeline, owners, KPI, conversion path | Does every channel have a clear job? |
| Production | Copy, landing page, email, social, ad creative, sales/support notes | Are assets consistent and complete? |
| Review | Brand, factual accuracy, compliance, accessibility, tracking | Is human approval needed before launch? |
| Scheduling | Send times, post times, ad launch windows, follow-up dates | Are deadlines realistic and conflicts removed? |
| Launch | Publish/send/activate assets | Are links, forms, events, and UTMs working? |
| Monitoring | Early performance, delivery issues, comments, replies, spend, conversion quality | Is anything broken or misleading? |
| Learning | Results, best assets, weak steps, repurposing opportunities | What should be kept, stopped, or tested next? |

### Human Review Rules

Require explicit human approval before publishing or sending when:

- Claims mention health, finance, legal, safety, pricing, guarantees, or regulated outcomes.
- The campaign uses customer names, testimonials, screenshots, or case studies.
- The content reacts to sensitive news, social issues, politics, disasters, or public controversy.
- The post is scheduled from an automated pipeline without manual inspection.
- The brand is early-stage and tone/positioning is still being defined.

### Dry-Run Mode

For any automated or semi-automated campaign workflow, run a dry run first:

- Generate the full content package without publishing.
- Produce the campaign calendar with scheduled dates/times.
- Validate links, UTMs, forms, and destination pages.
- Show the approval list by asset and channel.
- Identify anything blocked by missing permissions, credentials, assets, or policy.

### Content Pool And Reuse

When a campaign creates many assets, maintain a content pool:

| Field | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| `asset_id` | Stable identifier for the asset |
| `campaign` | Campaign name |
| `channel` | Email, social, paid, content, partner, sales |
| `asset_type` | Post, email, ad, landing section, script, one-pager |
| `message_pillar` | Pain, outcome, proof, objection, urgency, story |
| `status` | Idea, draft, review, approved, scheduled, live, retired |
| `owner` | Person or team responsible |
| `tracking_url` | URL with campaign tracking where relevant |
| `result` | Performance note after use |

This prevents one-off content from disappearing after launch and makes future campaigns faster.

## Optimisation During Campaign

Use the weakest-step diagnosis:

| Symptom | Likely Issue | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High impressions, low clicks | Message, creative, or targeting mismatch | Rewrite hook, improve offer clarity, test creative |
| High clicks, low conversion | Landing page or offer friction | Improve above-the-fold clarity, CTA, proof, form friction |
| Good conversion, poor lead quality | Wrong audience or incentive | Tighten qualification, adjust channel, change offer |
| Email opens but low clicks | Weak CTA or content mismatch | Make one CTA, sharpen reason to click, improve preview text |
| Social engagement but no traffic | Content not linked to conversion path | Add stronger CTA, retarget engagers, pin destination |
| Paid spend without learning | Tracking or test design problem | Reduce variables, fix events, compare by source/creative |

Optimisation rules:
- Change one major variable at a time when learning matters.
- Do not optimise only for cheap clicks if the goal is qualified conversion.
- Keep a changelog of major edits and dates.
- Preserve the best-performing creative for future campaigns.

## Guardrails

- Do not invent budgets, benchmarks, or claims. State assumptions clearly.
- Do not treat vanity metrics as success unless the user explicitly wants awareness.
- Do not launch without a defined conversion path and measurement plan.
- Do not recommend paid spend until tracking and landing page readiness are acceptable.
- Do not create urgency that is false or manipulative.
- Keep the campaign practical enough for the user's team size and timeline.
